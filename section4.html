Appendix C: Day Off Suggestions

[span_6](start_span)

Taking a day to rest and see more of the country is a healthy way to recharge.[span_6](end_span) [span_7](start_span)Here are a few popular and accessible options from Tanger.[span_7](end_span)

1. Chefchaouen (The Blue City)

[span_8](start_span)

Overview: Famous for its stunning blue-washed buildings nestled in the Rif Mountains, Chefchaouen is one of the most picturesque cities in Morocco.[span_8](end_span) [span_9](start_span)It's a relaxed place to wander through winding alleys, shop for local crafts, and enjoy a meal in a beautiful setting.[span_9](end_span)

Travel from Tanger:

    [span_10](start_span)
  • Bus (CTM): This is the most common and comfortable option.[span_10](end_span) The CTM bus company runs several trips a day. [span_11](start_span)The journey takes approximately 2-3 hours.[span_11](end_span) [span_12](start_span)It's best to book tickets a day or two in advance, especially during peak season.[span_12](end_span)
  • Grand Taxi: specify which stand to use

2. Asilah

[span_13](start_span)

Overview: A charming, fortified seaside town known for its art scene.[span_13](end_span) [span_14](start_span)The whitewashed walls of its old medina are decorated with colorful murals that change every year during the town's art festival.[span_14](end_span) [span_15](start_span)It's a great place for a relaxed day of walking, enjoying the ocean breeze, and eating fresh seafood.[span_15](end_span)

Travel from Tanger:

    [span_16](start_span)
  • Grand Taxi: The easiest way to get to Asilah.[span_16](end_span) [span_17](start_span)You can hire a whole taxi for a fixed price or share one with other passengers.[span_17](end_span) The trip takes about 45 minutes to an hour.
    • [span_18](start_span)
  • Train: There is a train station in Asilah, making it an easy and affordable trip from Tanger.[span_18](end_span) [span_19](start_span)The journey is also around 45 minutes to an hour.[span_19](end_span)

3. Tarifa, Spain

[span_20](start_span)

Overview: For a taste of Europe, the Spanish coastal town of Tarifa is just a short ferry ride away.[span_20](end_span) [span_21](start_span)It's famous for its vibrant kitesurfing and windsurfing scene, beautiful beaches, and laid-back Andalusian charm.[span_21](end_span) [span_22](start_span)You can explore the old town, watch the surfers, and enjoy Spanish tapas.[span_22](end_span) [span_23](start_span)Note: This requires crossing an international border into the Schengen Area, so ensure all team members have the necessary passport/visa eligibility.[span_23](end_span)

Travel from Tanger:

    [span_24](start_span)
  • Ferry: Fast ferries run regularly from the port in Tanger Ville directly to Tarifa.[span_24](end_span) The journey takes about one hour.
