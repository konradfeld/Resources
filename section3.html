Section 3: Post-Trip Debrief: Processing, Learning, and Launching

Objective

To guide the team in processing their experiences, extracting key ministry principles, creating a personal action plan for re-entry, and casting a vision for their continued involvement in God's work in North Africa.

Trainer's Note

This is a crucial 4-5 hour session designed for the day before the team departs. It's essential to set the expectation that this is not a quick wrap-up but a vital part of their learning and growth. Keep it interactive and create a safe space for honest reflection. Plan to have a final team dinner together after the session concludes.

Trainer's Script

(Start of Session)

"Welcome, team. I know you are physically tired from a full week, long days, and the time change, and you're probably already thinking about your travel home. I want to ask you to be fully present with us for these next few hours. This debrief is one of the most important parts of this trip. It's where we learn from what we've experienced and ask God how He wants to use it to shape us moving forward. Let's start in prayer..."

Module A: Personal Check-in (The Heart)

"First, let's go around and share some highlights. In just a sentence or two, what is one moment from this week where you saw God's goodness or felt His pleasure? Let's celebrate these together."

"Thank you for sharing those. Now, let's also make space for the things that feel weighty. What was a low point for you? A difficult moment, a disappointment, or something that's still heavy on your heart?"

"There is so much power in cultivating this rhythm in our lives-making space to celebrate God's work and immediately bringing the hard things to Him in prayer."

Module B: Celebrating the Fruit (The Impact)

"Now I want to focus on a few high-impact stories that represent the incredible things God was doing in and through you all this week. Let's reflect on these and then pray over the people involved."

  • "Think about Yusuf, the taxi driver. Remember the hunger and joy in his eyes. What would it mean for him, his wife, and his three kids if he went all-in for Jesus? The impact of your conversation goes far beyond just him; it's for his whole household, his oikos."
  • "Remember Raouia, the young woman who called herself an atheist. She was searching for a solution to her loneliness and was amazed by the grace in John 3:16. We haven't heard from her since, and that's normal. But a seed was planted. Let's pray that it takes root."
  • "And we celebrate Tarik, who had been seeking for a long time and, after hearing the good news clearly, chose to believe and be baptized! Let's pray for him to be like the fourth soil-that he would be fruitful and multiply, and not be choked out by the cares of this world."

"Beyond the stories, let's look at the numbers, because every number represents a person."

  • Conversations:
  • Prayer: Praise God that many got to encounter Jesus through prayer this week. May not see response yet, but many in M had someone to pray over them and years later they now believe.
  • Gospel Shares: Praise God that 29 got to hear for the very first time that God loves them, that they can have a relationship with the Creator that doesn't depend on them. It is not insignificant. God is not done with them. This is why we make it our ambition to share with people the very first time we meet them.
  • Repent & Believe:
  • Dunked: Praise God, this is how the kingdom grows. We go and share the gospel not knowing who God is going to bring into his kingdom. It's the same in the US and here. What a privilege, we got to announce his gospel to people who have never heard it.

"Let's pray over all these numbers-not just for the people we talked to, but for their families, friends, and coworkers who will be impacted."

Module C: A Framework for Learning (The Head)

"As we reflect on the week, I want to give us a biblical framework for how the Kingdom grows. Let's look at Mark, chapter 4, verses 26-29."

"Jesus tells us the Kingdom of God is like a man who scatters seed. He sleeps, he rises, and the seed just grows-he doesn't even know how. This parable gives us a simple, beautiful picture of our role and God's role."

What are the parts involved in this story:

  • The Seed: This is the Gospel.
  • The Soil: This is the heart of the person who hears it.
  • The Sower: That's us! Anyone in the Kingdom.
  • The Spirit: This is the magic ingredient. We can't make the seed grow. Only God can do that.
  • The Sickle: This represents gathering the harvest-bringing new believers into community.
  • Seasons

"A guy in India, Nathan Shank, was going through this parable with a farmer who had believed. They got all the 'S's and then the farmer stopped him and said it's really like this and began to draw."

"This isn't a complex strategy and it's not new; it's a simple picture of the example we have been given in scripture and has been a helpful framework for believers all over the world. We can use this framework, often called the 4 Fields, to learn from our week."

  1. Entry (Go): "How did we find open people? What worked well when starting conversations?"
  2. Gospel (Sow): "What did it feel like to share the Gospel? What tools were helpful? What did you learn?"
  3. Discipleship (Grow): "How did we see each other grow this week? How did we learn from each other?"
  4. Assembly (Gather): "The goal is to form healthy assemblies, or churches. The Bible describes the church not just as a community with a shared interest, but as a team with a shared goal. We need each other." (Church Circle)
  5. Leadership Development: A wise farmer reserves some of his seed for the next planting season. Investment in people making disciples.

Module D: Personal Action Plan (The Hands)

"This trip isn't just an event; it's part of God's plan for you. The joy you felt this week is found in obedience. So now, I want you to take some time to prayerfully reflect."

Prompts:

  • Lord, what area of my life do you want me to grow in based on this trip?
  • What is one step of obedience I need to take in the next 30 days?
  • Who is one person back home I need to share my experience with?
  • (If you love me, you'll obey me - not a reprimand, an encouragement. Start with loving Jesus, obedience will come)

"After you've had some time to write, I'd like for each of you to share one of your goals with the group. This is how we build accountability."

Module E: Vision for Continued Partnership (The Future)

"As we close, I want to bring our focus back to this place. Morocco has 38 million people. North Africa has 110 million. This region is one of the most gospel-impoverished places on earth. Healthy local churches are incredibly rare. But God is at work, and He has invited you to be a part of it."

"So what now? There are three simple ways you can stay involved:"

  • Pray: Commit to praying for North Africa. We can give you resources like newsletters or websites to help you pray specifically.
  • Give: Financially support the long-term work that is happening here.
  • Go: Consider how God might be calling you back-short-term or even long-term.

"Do you believe God can bring a harvest to North Africa? He has done it before in other parts of the world, and He can do it here. Jesus has invited us to labor with him. Not in vain, but in victory."

"Let's close our time by praying over the goals you've written down and for the people of North Africa."
